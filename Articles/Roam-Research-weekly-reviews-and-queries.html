Roam Research weekly reviews and queries

  • September 17th, 2020
  • 660 Words

    • Chaos and Entropy

    • Suppose you have watched one of Ali Abdaal's videos and now you want to make your own productivity system. You get yourself a To-do List manager, a calendar and tape it together and say that it is a good day's work. The next day you use it. Maybe you even use it another day. But at last on the third or the fourth day, your system is all cluttered. You don't know what is where and what you should do.
    • This happens due to entropy or chaos. But what is entropy I hear you ask? Basically, entropy is the tendency of a system to fall apart or get cluttered. You make a To-do list management system and use it for Quick Capture, in the course of a few days, there are hundreds of rogue tasks which have been completed but haven't been checked off, there are tasks which are mega urgent but you can't find it.
    • Weekly Reviews are a process which you can use to prevent this from happening. They are the guardians of your system. They counter entropy. You can do these in any system. Heck it may even be with pen and paper. You just sit down on a day for about an hour and go through all of your system. Got a lot of rogue to-dos? Put them in a list. Got a lot of uncategorized tasks? Categorize them. Also, ask yourselves a few questions like "What went well this week?" or "What didn't go so well this week and how can I improve it next week?". These help you progress. If you maintain a journal, then go through all the entries for the week. Take notes on them too if you are into that. Now, lets get into how I do it using Roam Research.

    Queries

    • So I do maintain a journal and therefore, I also take notes on it. It gives you a lot of joy when you see what your a week younger self was thinking about. Now, I use one of Roam's most powerful features. Queries! It is the language that you talk to Roam with. Here is the query that I use.

    • Wait wait don't get scared. Let me explain.
    • First and foremost, the {{[[query]]: part only tells Roam that there is a query coming up.
    • Then we have {and: [[Input-1]] [[Input-2]] [[Input-3]]...}
    • This shows only the items which have all the criterion satisfied.
    • There is also the {or: [[Input-1]] [[Input-2]] [[Input-3]]...} which shows only the items which have at least one of the criterion satisfied.
    • At last there is {between: [[Date1]] [[Date2]]} which takes in two inputs as Dates. It will basically get all the daily notes day pages between those two dates inclusive.
    • Here is one of my favourite abilities of queries, you can nest them!! You can have something like {and: {or: [[Input-1]] [[Input-2]] [[Input-3]]...} {or: [[Input-4]] [[Input-5]] [[Input-6]]...}}
    • Lets break this down. What do you think this will do? It will display all the blocks which have been tagged with or are nested within ([[Input-1]] or [[Input-2]] or [[Input-3]] or ...) and ([[Input-4]] [[Input-5]] [[Input-6]]...)
    • Now that is cool!!
    • So lets breakdown my query for the weekly review
    • {and: {or: } {between: }}
    • Now this will go for anything that is in the {or: } part AND is between the two dates.
